Executive Summary

Taiwan's ODM industry stands at a critical inflection point. CES 2026 reveals a market splitting in two directions: the traditional PC business facing consolidation pressure, while AI infrastructure manufacturing explodes into a $32B+ opportunity.

The ODMs that thrive will be those that recognize this moment for what it is: not just a market shift, but a capability transformation imperative. Liquid cooling expertise, high-density power delivery, custom interconnect design—these are the new table stakes for AI infrastructure manufacturing.

The traditional assembly-focused business model is commoditizing. Value creation is migrating toward system integration, co-design partnerships, and specialized manufacturing capabilities.

Key Takeaways

  • AI server manufacturing is the growth engine—$32B+ and growing
  • PC brand consolidation increases customer concentration risk
  • Multi-vendor chip competition benefits Taiwan ecosystem
  • Capability transformation required: thermal, power, interconnect

Growth Vectors

The primary growth engine. Every hyperscaler expansion, every enterprise AI deployment needs Taiwan ODM manufacturing.

What This Means for You

Strategic questions based on your current position.

If You're a Large ODM (Foxconn, Quanta, Wistron scale)

You have scale advantages and established hyperscaler relationships. The question is execution speed on capability transformation.

  • • How quickly can you build liquid cooling and advanced thermal capabilities?
  • • Are you capturing your fair share of the AI infrastructure boom?
  • • How are you managing PC customer concentration as brands consolidate?

If You're a Mid-Tier ODM (Compal, Pegatron scale)

The middle position requires clear strategic choices. You can't compete on pure scale—differentiation and specialization matter more.

  • • What's your AI infrastructure entry strategy? Partnership? Acquisition?
  • • Which growth vectors (wearables, robotics, displays) match your capabilities?
  • • How do you compete for hyperscaler business against larger peers?

If You're a Specialized Manufacturer

Specialization can be an advantage in a fragmenting market. Deep expertise in specific categories creates defensible positions.

  • • How can you leverage specialized expertise for AI infrastructure niches?
  • • Are there underserved customer segments you can dominate?
  • • What partnerships could extend your capabilities without losing focus?
