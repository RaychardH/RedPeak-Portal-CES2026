Three Competitive Narratives

CES 2026 reveals three distinct positioning strategies for AI leadership.

Technology Leadership

NVIDIA, AMD, Intel

"We build the AI infrastructure"

Infrastructure Owners

These companies compete on compute performance, silicon innovation, and developer ecosystem control. NVIDIA leads with CUDA dominance while AMD bets on open ecosystems.

NVIDIA Full-stack AI, CUDA lock-in, Vera Rubin platform
AMD ROCm open-source, Helios rack-scale, cost advantage
Intel x86 compatibility, 18A process, hybrid AI

Ecosystem Scale

Samsung, LG

"We integrate AI into daily life"

Experience Orchestrators

Consumer electronics giants leverage installed base and ecosystem breadth. Samsung leads with 430M SmartThings devices; LG differentiates with "Affectionate Intelligence."

Samsung Vision AI, 500M devices/year, SmartThings ecosystem
LG Affectionate Intelligence, CLOiD robot, Wallpaper TV

Platform Integration

Lenovo

"We are the neutral AI platform"

Vendor-Agnostic Integrator

Lenovo uniquely positions as the neutral platform that works with all chip vendors (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel, Qualcomm). The only company with strategic partnerships across all major silicon providers.

Lenovo Kira AI, all-partner ecosystem, Sphere venue innovation
Strategy 8 partner executives on stage — most of any keynote

Eight Cross-Cutting Themes

Universal patterns that emerged across all 15 keynotes (P0, P1, P2).

Hybrid AI Consensus

Local + Cloud wins

All keynotes converged on hybrid AI — local compute for privacy/performance, cloud for complex reasoning. Competition shifts to orchestration quality.

Personal AI Race

AI agents emerge

Lenovo Kira, Samsung Vision AI, LG CLOiD, Cat AI Assistant — personal AI agents across consumer and industrial categories. First-mover advantage available.

Robotics Moment

Concept to reality

Boston Dynamics Atlas, LG CLOiD, NVIDIA Cosmos, Caterpillar autonomy. Hyundai committing 30K robots by 2028. Industrial robotics is real; consumer 2-3 years out.

Open vs. Proprietary

Ecosystem strategy

NVIDIA CUDA proprietary vs. AMD ROCm open-source. Lenovo betting on openness. LEGO's screenless SMART Play as counter to app-dependent IoT.

Scale Competition

Numbers as narrative

NVIDIA "220T transistors", AMD "100x by 2030", Samsung "500M devices/year", Caterpillar "11B tons moved" — scale metrics as competitive moat.

Industrial AI Rise

B2B transformation

NEW from P1

Siemens Industrial AI OS + NVIDIA, Caterpillar autonomous fleet, digital twins. B2B AI deployments scaling faster than consumer. $30B R&D commitments.

Experience Innovation

Venue as differentiator

NEW from P1

Delta + Lenovo at Sphere, LEGO on Exosphere, immersive wind/aroma effects. Keynote venue selection now a strategic brand expression choice.

Mobility Convergence

EV + Entertainment

NEW from P1

Sony Honda AFEELA as "Creative Entertainment Space", PlayStation in-car, Delta + Uber SkyMiles. Mobility becoming entertainment platform, not just transport.
