OUR POINT OF VIEW

When Technology
Commoditizes, Brand
Becomes the Moat

Five strategic observations from CES 2026 that will reshape how brands compete, differentiate, and connect.

January 2026 · RedPeak Strategy Team

CES is not just a technology showcase—it's a leading indicator of where brand strategy must evolve. After analyzing 40+ major brands and 11 industry hypotheses validated against CES 2026 announcements, we've identified five themes that demand strategic attention.

These aren't predictions. They're observations about shifts already underway—and strategic questions every brand leader should be asking.

THEME 01

The Brand Differentiation Crisis

When every laptop has an NPU, every TV has AI upscaling, and every phone has computational photography—what's left to differentiate on?

AI is now table stakes

Intel's 180 TOPS, AMD's 60 TOPS, Qualcomm's 80 TOPS—NPU specs have converged.

Feature parity is instant

Any innovation gets copied within one product cycle. Specs-based differentiation has a half-life of months.

Consumers aren't buying AI

Dell's own admission: "Consumers are not buying based on AI." The spec war has disconnected from actual purchase drivers.

"

The brands that win in 2026 won't be the ones with the best specs. They'll be the ones with the clearest story about why those specs matter to real people.

Strategic Question

What's your differentiation story beyond specifications?

THEME 02

China's Brand Maturation Moment

Chinese brands are no longer competing on price alone. They're winning on innovation, earning recognition, and closing the technology gap.

100+ Innovation Awards

Chinese brands earned 40% of total CES awards—TCL alone won 50+.

Technical excellence achieved

TCL's 10,000 nit display, Hisense's RGB MiniLED—spec parity is here.

Brand premium still elusive

Technical excellence ≠ brand equity. Samsung and Apple still command price premiums.

"

The quality gap has closed. The brand gap remains—but for how long? The question now is whether Chinese brands can build premium brand equity to match their technical excellence.

Strategic Question

If you're an established brand—how will you compete when Chinese competitors match your technology at lower price points?

THEME 03

Physical AI: The Next Brand Battlefield

AI is escaping the screen. Robots, smart home agents, and embodied AI systems are creating entirely new categories—and new brand experience canvases.

LG's AI Agent Home

A vision of AI agents orchestrating your entire living environment.

Samsung's Agentic Home

AI that anticipates needs and acts autonomously across devices.

NVIDIA World Foundation

Physical AI as the next computing platform—from gaming to industrial.

"

When AI has a physical presence, brand experience extends beyond screens into spaces. Trust and safety become core brand differentiators, not just features.

Strategic Question

How does your brand show up when AI enters physical spaces?

THEME 04

The Sustainability Authenticity Gap

Sustainability has moved from marketing claim to architectural imperative. The gap between stated values and actual practices is becoming verifiable.

Verification is now possible

Third-party certifications and carbon tracking make claims auditable.

Certifications drive purchasing

EPEAT, Energy Star becoming procurement requirements.

Material innovation leads

Lenovo's 50% recycled aluminum, ASUS's Ceraluminum™.

"

The brands leading at CES 2026—Lenovo, ASUS, Samsung, LG—are building sustainability into product architecture, not just marketing copy. Consumers are paying attention.

Strategic Question

Is your sustainability story built into product architecture—or just marketing copy?

THEME 05

Experience Economy 3.0

We've moved from products (1.0) to ecosystems (2.0). Now AI-orchestrated experiences across devices create entirely new forms of brand interaction.

1.0

Product Era

Compete on features, specs, and price. Brand is secondary to product performance.

2.0

Ecosystem Era

Lock-in through device interconnection. Brand creates switching costs through integration.

3.0

Experience Era

AI agents orchestrate personalized experiences. Brand becomes the experience layer.

"

In Experience Economy 3.0, your brand isn't what you say—it's how your AI agent behaves. Every automated interaction is a brand moment.

Strategic Question

What does your brand personality look like when expressed through autonomous AI agents?
