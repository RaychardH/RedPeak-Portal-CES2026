Executive Summary

CES 2026 marks the moment when AI capabilities in consumer electronics reached commodity status. Every major chip vendor now ships NPUs with comparable performance—Intel at 180 TOPS, Qualcomm at 80 TOPS, AMD at 60 TOPS. The AI PC positioning race is effectively over.

For electronics brands, this creates an urgent strategic question: When technology specifications converge, what becomes your competitive moat?

The market is consolidating rapidly. Winners are pulling ahead through ecosystem play, premium positioning, and genuine innovation. Mid-tier brands face an existential choice: move up to premium or compete on value—the middle is disappearing.

Key Takeaways

  • AI specs no longer differentiate—all vendors have "enough" NPU power
  • Premium (+15%) and value (+12%) segments growing; mid-tier (-5%) squeezed
  • Chinese brands won 40% of CES Innovation Awards—quality parity achieved
  • Sustainability moved from marketing to product architecture requirement

Strategic Imperatives

Four priorities for electronics brands navigating the post-AI-commoditization landscape.

IMPERATIVE 01

Differentiate Beyond Specs

When every competitor has comparable AI capabilities, differentiation must come from brand experience, design excellence, and ecosystem value.

Build distinctive brand experiences that transcend product features
Invest in design language that creates emotional connection
Develop software/service layers that add genuine value

BENCHMARK

Apple's ecosystem lock-in, ASUS's design innovation, Lenovo's enterprise credibility

IMPERATIVE 02

Pick Your Lane: Premium or Value

The mid-tier is being squeezed from both ends. Successful brands are making decisive moves toward either premium differentiation or value optimization.

Cross-device experiences (phone + laptop + tablet + wearable)

What This Means for You

Strategic questions based on your market position.

If You're a Premium Brand Leader

Your position is strong but not unassailable. Chinese competitors are closing the quality gap rapidly.

  • • How will you maintain price premium when feature parity arrives?
  • • Is your ecosystem lock-in strong enough to resist switching?
  • • Are you investing enough in brand experience vs. product features?

If You're in the Squeezed Middle

The most urgent strategic situation. Mid-tier brands are losing ground to both premium and value competitors.

  • • Can you credibly move upmarket, or is value optimization the path?
  • • What's your differentiation story when you can't compete on price or prestige?
  • • Are there niche segments (gaming, creator, sustainability) where you can dominate?

If You're Competing on Value

Value competition is intensifying with Chinese brand quality parity. Scale and efficiency become critical.

  • • How will you compete with Chinese brands that have cost structure advantages?
  • • Can you build brand equity while maintaining value positioning?
  • • Are there geographic or channel advantages you can leverage?
